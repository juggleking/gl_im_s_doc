Communication between Glims and external systems

Contents: Communication design - Drivers - Services - MIPS internal protocol - Direct communication

Communication design

GLIMS communicates with external applications, such as a HIS (Hospital Information System), result servers, OMR scanners or analyzers, through an intermediate process called a driver.

  1. Drivers translate messages from the external application's language into a MIPS internal protocol, and vice versa.

  2. GLIMS services exchange information between the translators and the database.

Drivers

Drivers are stand-alone applications that translate messages from the external application's language into a MIPS internal protocol, and vice versa. They are considered as separate products, each with their own release cycle. This implies that drivers are not documented in the GLIMS manual but come with a dedicated driver manual which is available on our Customer Service Center website. Among other, the driver documentation will contain specific options and the communication mapping between the external software and the MIPS internal protocol.

In GLIMS, drivers are defined as "translators". Click here a full description on translator configuration.

Services

GLIMS services exchange information between the translators and the database. Each service can handle several translators simultaneously. For optimal performance, the amount of translators per service should be limited.

In GLIMS, these services are defined as "services (of type Translator Server)". Click here a full description on service configuration.

MIPS internal protocol

MIPS uses an internal protocol as an intermediate step for

  1. processing external protocol messages in the GLIMS database
  2. sending messages in an external protocol

In both cases, it is the driver that makes the translation between the internal protocol and the external protocol. The services are responsible for handling incoming messages formatted in the internal protocol or sending messages in the internal protocol format.

MIPS uses two different types of internal protocols:

  1. A MIPS-specific ASTM protocol that is used for analyzer communication
  2. A "URL-formatted" internal protocol that is used for any other type of communication.

Direct communication

There are some exceptions to this communication design. In some cases, GLIMS allows to generate messages/files that are directly formatted into an external protocol. In these cases, there is no driver involved that makes the translation between the GLIMS internal protocol and the external protocol.
