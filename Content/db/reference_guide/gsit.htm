Security > Password strength

Level of password strength (medium or high).
The rules for medium are:
- Password should be at least the configured number of characters. This configuration can be done on user level or the entire application.
- Password should be different than the previous one.
- Password shouldn't be used recently.

The rules for high are:
- At least 9 characters long (configuration is not taken into account)
- Password should contain characters from three of the four following categories:
- Uppercase letters of European languages (A through Z, without diacritic marks, Greek and Cyrillic characters) A, B, C, ? Z
- Lowercase letters of European languages (a through z, sharp-s, without diacritic marks, Greek and Cyrillic characters) a, b, c, ? z
- Base 10 digits (0 through 9) 0, 1, 2, ? 9
- Non-alphanumeric characters (special characters) $,!,%,^,(){}[];:<>?

Currently Diacritic marks, Greek and Cyrillic characters are NOT taken into account, neither are Unicode characters from Asian languages.

Mandatory PasswordStrength PasswordStrength

One of: Medium, High

Station Station
The station for which we want to scan specimens. THis can be a station specifically created to serve as scan station.
When the station correspondends with a Specimen Archive, the program swithces to an appropriate mode where only rack defined for this Specimen Archive are accepted and where the specimens are automatically assigned to racks via Specimen Storage records. For this mode, the Specimen Archive license is required
String StorageType
Ignore: only the fact that the specimen was scanned is stored. The specimen. HomeLocation and StorageLocation are not adapted and the specimen is not added to any archive rack. Usefull for simple scans to print labels or check a specimen's status without actually putting the specimen in a rack.
Home: the scan position is written in the Specimen.HomeLocation and the specimen is added to the archive rack (if applicable). Usefull for assigning a central dispatch position to a specimen.
Storage: the scan position is written in the Specimen.StorageLocation and the specimen is added to the archive rack (if applicable). Usefull for adding the specimen to a long-term archive.
Rack: the specimen is simply added to the archive rack (if applicable). The Specimen.HomeLocation and Specimen.StorageLocation fields are not overwritten. Usefull for putting specimens in an intermediary storage rack.
