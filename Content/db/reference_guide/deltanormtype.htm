Type of a delta norm:
Linear: the result can vary with an absolute value of <a> or a percentage <p> per <lapse>. I.e., after 2 * <lapse>, the allowed deviation is 2 * <a> or 2 * <p> percent
Exponential: the result can decrease with <y> % per <lapse>. I.e. when the initial value is x0 = 100, p = 50%, and <lapse> = 1, then the limit after 1 day is 50, the limit after 2 days is 25, etc...
The formula used is: Limit = <X0> * exp(1 + <p> / 100, <time lapse> / <lapse denominator>), where <p> is typically negative
