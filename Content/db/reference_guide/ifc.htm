A patient infection is a group of isolations detected in the same patient and belonging to the same 'strain' or 'antibiotype', i.e.
- same microorganism
- same patient
- similar antibiogram
For isolations which are detected much later - outside of the infection period - but with the similar antibiogram etc. (the so-called RE-INFECTIONS), there is a reference from the re-infection to the primary infection.
