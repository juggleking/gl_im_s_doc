BTM - Blood bag typings

As of GLIMS 6.0, the blood group and the rhesus are directly stored in the blood bag itself. You should no longer use the typing table for this. This implies that when a blood bag is created with a certain 'typing', all information of that typing (blood group, rhesus and antigens) is immediately copied to the blood bag. During routine functions, the characteristics of the typing are no longer used.

When using ISBT-128 the barcodes that contain blood group, rhesus and phenotype information are decoded by GLIMS and therefore do not need the Typing table. When the applicable typing still exists in the Typing table, GLIMS still puts a reference from the blood bag to this typing (just like before). It also checks if the typing's attributes correspond to the ISBT-128 definitions.

New customers do not have to create Typing records if they use

  • ISBT-128 barcodes, or
  • delivery disks to import the blood bag data.

The blood bag typing table merely exists as a 'translation table' for non ISBT-codes.
