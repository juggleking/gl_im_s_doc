Steps and status description

1. Blood bag record creation

The very first action a blood bag can be subject to is the creation of it in the blood bag database. This can either be carried out manually or by scanning the barcodes. In either case, after creation, the blood bag will attain the status Initial, indicating that for the moment no regular reservation, i.e. blood selection, is made for this blood bag.
Notes

Notes

  • As explained in the chapter Blood bag verification, a blood bag can have status Verification before reaching the status Initial.
  • A blood bag that attains status Initial can be referred to by blood selections. These blood selections however will not be regular ones; they must have been discontinued in the past.

2. Blood request and blood selection creation

To be able to make a reservation for a blood bag, one needs to carry out a blood request. This will automatically create a blood selection, attaining status Initial. It means that at the moment no blood bag has been assigned to the blood selection.

3. Assignment of compatible blood bag to blood selection

The next step is to search for a (compatible) blood bag. Once such a blood bag is found, the blood selection will refer to the chosen blood bag. Both will be promoted with respect to their status, namely, the blood selection and blood bag will attain status Reserved. The first promotion indicates that a reservation has been done, while the latter reflects that the particular blood bag has been selected as candidate for at least one selection.

4. Cross match

The next step is to check the compatibility in a physical way, the so-called cross match.
Note that one must be able to distinguish whether or not a blood bag has been subject to this latter check. To indicate whether a blood bag has passed the physical compatibility check, the blood selection and blood bag can attain the status Checked.

5. Delivery of the blood bag for transfusion

Once a blood bag has been physically checked, it is ready to be distributed to the proper ward where one requires it. As this happen, the blood bag will leave the laboratory. To indicate that a blood bag has left the laboratory, the status Checked out is used. The corresponding blood selection needs to attain a new status too, because the actions required to select a blood bag, i.e. reservation and the compatibility check, are done. Therefore, the blood selection will be promoted to status Ready.

6. Transfusion

A blood bag attaining status Checked out is no longer available in the laboratory and will normally be transfused. To distinguish between the first and the latter state, the blood bag will attain Administered to indicate that the person has actually received the blood.

Discontinuation

Note that in the above selection and transfusion scheme, one assumes that the blood bag has to be transfused. Yet, one can imagine that a request made for a blood selection is no longer required. Then the blood selection can be assigned the status Discontinued. This promotion however is restricted to blood selections attaining states from Initial up to Ready (the latter only if the blood bag has status Checked out).
Not only a blood selection but also a blood bag can be Discontinued. This is the case when something happens that makes the blood bag unusable for transfusions (dropped out of the refrigerator, heated too fast, …).

Expiration

A blood bag contains a material that has a limited lifetime indicated by its expiration time. If that time has elapsed, the blood bag cannot be used anymore. To mark this particular event, the blood bag is assigned the status Expired.
