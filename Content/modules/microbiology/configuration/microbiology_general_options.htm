Introduction

The microbiology and epidemiology modules can be tailored to the needs of a site using the general settings under Start -> System management -> Customize -> GLIMS microbiology. These settings are presented below.

Title bar

Specimen-based text used for the title bar of the microbiology work screen.

Keep whole antibiogram when organism is changed

When changing the organism of an isolation in the microbiology work screen (change from one organism into another organism), GLIMS will by default automatically remove all antibiogram cells for that isolation that did not have a value yet. Depending on the new organism, another antibiogram can be added automatically as well. However some labs prefer that the empty antibiogram cells are NOT removed automatically when the organism is changed. This can be accomplished by enabling this option.

More

Method to search for encounter and stay

This option applies to the microbiology action query, the isolation query and all statistics programs (microbiology, production, financial, transfusion). Possible options:

Only look at order's encounter

GLIMS only looks at the encounter and the ward that were specified when the order was entered. Other encounters that were active at that time are ignored.

Don't look at order's encounter

GLIMS does not look which encounter and ward were specified when the order was entered. GLIMS searches for the most appropriate encounter and corresponding stay that were active at the date and time that the specimen was sampled.

When multiple encounters were active at that time, priority is first given to hospitalized encounters, else to encounters containing an appropriate stay (of course this search might well result in the same encounter and stay as those that were specified in the order).

First look at order's encounter

GLIMS first looks at the encounter and the ward that were specified when the order was entered. If none were specified, GLIMS does the same search as for Don't look at order's encounter.
