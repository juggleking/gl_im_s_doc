Anatomic pathology - Introduction

A pathology examination does not differ much from a regular chemistry analysis except from the fact that for a pathology examination most results will be texts (but integer values and images or photo's can also be included in a pathology examination) and analysis will be done by a pathologist in stead of a station. Each pathology examination will also have a conclusion.

There are 3 different examinations that occur in the pathology:

Histological examinations, also called biopsy examinations

Here biopsy specimens of any part of the body can be examined.

Cytology examinations

This can be a cervix of a fornix examination or a combination of both.

Autopsy

This resembles a histological examination but with biopsy specimens from all over the body.

A pathology examination consists of four main steps:

  • The description of the received material (this can be macroscopic and microscopic) that should be examined.
  • The description of some clinical information which can supply extra info for the examination.
  • The description of the results.
  • The conclusion of the examination.
