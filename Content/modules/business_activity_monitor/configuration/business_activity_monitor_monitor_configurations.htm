Business Activity Monitor - Monitor configurations

Introduction

Sets of parameters can be configured to limit the data displayed in the General monitor and TAT monitor to that of a specific

  • requesting department
  • property classification
  • executing department group
  • executing department
  • minimal urgency
  • workplace
  • domain

These sets of parameters are called Monitor configurations and are available via Start -> Business activity monitor -> Monitor parameter sets.

At least one monitor configuration should be created, otherwise the Business Activity Monitor will not be displayed.

The defined monitor configurations can then be selected in the upper left corner of the General monitor and TAT monitor:

Fields

The Monitor configuration editor looks as follows:

Each field is described below. The Name field is the only mandatory field.

Name

Name that is used to refer to the monitor configuration in the monitor configuration selection list of the General monitor or TAT monitor.

Department

If a requesting Department is specified,

  • The results counted in the BAM are those that belong to orders that were requested in the indicated department.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those that were requested in the indicated department.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose originating Requested code belongs to an order requested in the indicated department.

If no department is specified, the results, orders and actions counted in the BAM are those of all the requesting departments of the laboratory.

Classification

If a property Classification is specified, the results counted in the BAM are those of that classification. If no property classification is specified, the results counted are those of all the properties belonging to the default property classification.

Executing department group

If an Executing department group is specified,
  • The results counted in the BAM are those whose Executing department belongs to the indicated executing department group.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those which contain at least one result whose Executing department belongs to the indicated executing department group.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose Procedure has, as Department, a department that belongs to the indicated executing department group.

Executing department

If an Executing department is specified,
  • The results counted in the BAM are those whose Executing department is the indicated executing department.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those which contain at least one result whose Executing department is the indicated executing department.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose Procedurehas the indicated executing department as Department.

Minimal urgency

If a Minimal urgency is specified,
  • The results counted in the BAM are those whose Urgency is equal or superior to the indicated minimal urgency.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those that contain at least one result whose Urgency is equal or superior to the indicated minimal urgency.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose Urgency is equal or superior to the indicated minimal urgency.

Workplace

If a Workplace is specified,
  • The results counted in the BAM are those whose output has a Procedure output (of type Property) that is linked to an assessment method associated with a Station that belongs to the indicated workplace.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those which contain at least one result whose output has a Procedure output (of type Property) that is linked to an assessment method associated with a Station that belongs to the indicated workplace.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose procedure has a Station that belongs to the indicated workplace.

Domain

If a Domain is specified,
  • The results counted in the BAM are those whose property has the indicated domain as Domain.
  • The orders counted in the BAM are those which contain at least one result whose property has the indicated domain as Domain.
  • The actions counted in the BAM are those whose Procedurehas, as output, a property that has the indicated domain as Domain.
