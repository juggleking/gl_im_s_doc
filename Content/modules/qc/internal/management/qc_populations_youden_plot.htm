Youden plot

Introduction

The QC program in GLIMS can show a Youden plot of 2 selected qc populations via the QCpopulation.YoudenPlot() function or the  QCPopulation.LeveyJenningsGraph() function.

Generating a Youden plot

For this type of graph, you should select 2 QC populations which have a number of results from the same dates and shifts.  You can start by selecting 2 populations, call the youden plot (in the Levey-Jennins screen) and then click on another population in the browser. In the first phase, explicitly selected record 1 and 2 are compared. After clicking on the new record, the first selected and lastly clicked population are compared.

The program takes the result of each shift from the first population and tries to match it up with a result from the same date and shift from the second population. All these points are shown in a scatter graph, where the X-axis shows the normalized deviations to QCPopulation.TargetMean of the first population and the Y-axis shows the normalized deviations for the second population.  The correlation coefficient between the two groups of results is also shown. The closer this coefficient is to 1, the stronger the (positive) correlation between the 2 populations.

Note: direct printing of the graph is not yet supported. The print button in the graph itself allows manual printing to your current MS-Windows default printer.
