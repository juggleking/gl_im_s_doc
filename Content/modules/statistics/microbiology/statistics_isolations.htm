Statistics -> Isolation statistics

Isolation statistics give an overview on what microorganisms have been isolated in the lab. It allows to answer questions like:

  • How many germs (per group) were detected in a certain ward ? How did this number change in time ?
  • Is (was) there an epidemic in (a) certain ward(s), in a certain hospital, ... ?
  • Does the resistance to a specific antibiotic increase ?
  • Which patient categories show which microorganisms ?
  • ...

Layout screen

Classification options for rows/columns/multi-table are

All time options

Refer to the time the specimen was taken from the patient.

Material

Of the specimen.

Primary material variable

Value of the primary material variable of the specimen (method, origin, ...); which variable is the primary depends on the variables defined on the material and their sequence number; the primary is the one with the lowest sequence number.

Ward

Where the patient stayed at the time of specimen sampling.

Intensive care

Make a division between patients that resided in a ward of type 'ICU' (intensive care unit), patients that resided in another ward, and patients that resided in no ward (at the date of sampling).

Issuer

Of the order the specimen belongs to (can be doctor, department, ward, ...).

Microorganism

That was isolated

Microorganism group

To which the isolated organism hierarchically belongs; a microorganism group is a microorganism where the 'Is group' flag is switched on in the microorganism editor; all microorganisms which have this microorganism as parent or grand-parent etc. belong to this group

Positive or negative

Was the appraisal (quantity) marked as positive ?

Severity

Microbiology procedure
